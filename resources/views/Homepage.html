Lechon Paksiw

Lechon Paksiw is a popular Filipino dish made by simmering leftover roasted pig (lechon) in a flavorful sauce. The sauce typically consists of vinegar, soy sauce, lechon sauce (or liver sauce), garlic, onions, and spices. The tangy and slightly sweet flavors of the dish come from the vinegar and lechon sauce combination.

It’s an economical and delicious way to repurpose leftovers while maintaining the rich, savory taste of the original lechon. Best paired with steamed rice!

Arroz Caldo

Arroz Caldo is a comforting Filipino rice porridge that is perfect for rainy days or when you’re feeling under the weather. It’s made by simmering rice in a chicken broth until it becomes creamy, then flavored with garlic, ginger, and fish sauce.

Topped with boiled egg, fried garlic, green onions, and sometimes calamansi, it has a hearty and savory taste with a hint of warmth from the ginger. Often served with a squeeze of calamansi and a side of fried tofu or tokwa’t baboy.

Pinakbet

Pinakbet is a traditional Filipino vegetable dish originating from the northern regions of the Philippines. It is made with a mix of local vegetables like eggplant, squash, bitter melon (ampalaya), okra, and string beans. These are sautéed and simmered in a shrimp paste (bagoong alamang) sauce, giving it a salty and umami-rich flavor.

Sometimes, pork or shrimp is added for extra depth. It’s a nutritious dish that pairs wonderfully with rice and is a staple in many Filipino households.
